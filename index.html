Mahatma Gandhi

1869-1948

Father of India

Mahatma Gandhi, known as the father of the Indian nation, was a pivotal freedom fighter and social reformer. He championed non-violent resistance and played a key role in India's struggle for independence from British rule. Gandhi promoted the use of Swadeshi goods, boycotting foreign products to boost local industries. His teachings extended beyond politics, advocating for social equality and challenging societal norms. Gandhi's simple lifestyle and profound wisdom earned him admiration worldwide. He led significant movements like the Quit India movement in 1942, leaving an indelible mark on history.
